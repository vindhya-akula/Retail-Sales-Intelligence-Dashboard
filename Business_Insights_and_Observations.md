# Business Insights and Observations

## KPI Analysis

### Total Sales

#### Business Purpose:
Measures the total revenue generated by the business during the analysis period. It helps management evaluate overall sales performance, monitor business growth, and compare revenue across different products, regions, and time periods.


### Total Profit

#### Business Purpose:
Measures the total profit earned by the business after sales. It helps management evaluate business profitability, understand financial performance, and identify opportunities to improve profit through pricing, cost control, or sales strategies.

### Total Orders

#### Business Purpose:
Measures the total number of orders placed by customers. It helps management understand customer purchasing activity, monitor business demand, and evaluate overall order volume over time.

### Total Customers

#### Business Purpose:
Measures the total number of unique customers who made purchases. It helps management evaluate customer reach, understand the size of the active customer base, and assess customer acquisition and retention performance.

### Average Rating

#### Business Purpose:
Measures the average customer satisfaction rating for purchased products. It helps management evaluate product quality, customer experience, and identify areas that require improvement to increase customer satisfaction and loyalty.

### Average Order Value(AOV)

#### Business Purpose:
Measures the average amount spent by customers in each order. It helps management understand customer spending behavior, evaluate sales performance, and develop strategies to increase the value of each purchase through promotions, product bundles, or cross-selling.

### Profit Margin

#### Business Purpose:
Measures the percentage of profit earned from total sales revenue. It helps management evaluate business efficiency, monitor overall profitability, and determine whether sales growth is generating healthy financial returns.

## Business Report
## Monthly Sales Trend

-Observation:
Sales increased from February to April, where April recorded the highest sales. After April, sales gradually declined, with a significant drop in July. Sales remained relatively stable from August to December.

-Business Insight:
The business experienced strong sales performance during the first half of the year, particularly in April. However, a sharp decline in July suggests possible seasonal demand changes, reduced promotional activities, inventory shortages, or other operational factors that require further investigation.

-Recommendation:
Management should investigate the factors contributing to the strong sales performance in April and identify the reasons for the significant decline in July. After validating the causes, successful strategies can be replicated during lower-performing months to improve annual sales performance.
